Stats

Together, we can build a more sustainable future for everyone



Waste and consumption statistics

  • Household waste: In 2019,the average Irish household generated 330 kg of waste, an increase from the previous two years.
  • 40% of household waste was placed in residual (black) bins.
  • 16% of household waste was collected for recycling (green bin).
  • 10% was collected in brown bins for organic waste.




  • Food waste: Ireland produced about 1.1 million tonnes of food waste in 2019, with 23% of it coming from households.
  • The average Irish household throws out an estimated 150 kg of food waste each year, costing approximately €700.




  • Municipal waste: Ireland’s municipal waste recycling rate was 37% in 2019, a slight decrease from 38% in 2018.
  • Toxic chemicals: Between 2018 and 2023, the EU’s consumption of hazardous chemicals decreased by 24.1%.
  • Material footprint: Ireland's material footprint per capita increased from 25.8 kg in 2010 to 49.3 kg in 2019, although the material footprint per GDP decreased over the same period.


Emissions and transport statistics

Fossil fuel subsidies: Ireland's fossil fuel subsidies decreased by 23% from 2019 to 2020, totaling €2.2 billion.

Car emissions: The average CO2 emissions per km for new passenger cars in the EU decreased by 26.1% between 2018 and 2023, reaching 107.6 g/km.

However, these reductions are not yet sufficient to meet 2030 targets, which aim for a further decrease in emissions.

The market share of zero-emission vehicles (ZEVs) in the EU increased from 1% in 2018 to 14.5% in 2023, though this varies significantly by country.







Global perspective

  • Population growth: The global population is projected to reach 9.8 billion by 2050, increasing the urgency for sustainable consumption and production patterns.
  • Food waste: Globally, the EPA estimates that the United States generated 66 million tons of wasted food in the food retail, food service, and residential sectors in 2019.
