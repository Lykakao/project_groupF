SDG12

Together, we can build a more sustainable future for everyone

SDG12 is the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. This goal promotes the efficient use of natural resources, reduces waste through recycling and reuse, encourages businesses to adopt sustainable practices, and educates the public on sustainable lifestyles.



Key principles of responsible production



  • Waste reduction: A primary focus is on preventing, reducing, recycling, and reusing waste to significantly minimize it.
  • Resource efficieny: It emphasizes the efficient use of natural resources, moving away from a linear "take-make-dispose" model towards a more circular economy.
  • Sustainability in the supply chain: Companies can shift away from fossil fuels and reduce emissions throughout their entire production process.
  • Innovation and technology: The use of advanced technology can help create more resource-efficient and sustainable products and services.
  • Ethical manufacturing: This includes ensuring fair and ethical practices within the manufacturing process itself.




How it is achieved

  • Government policies: Governments can implement policies and monitoring to encourage sustainable practices.
  • Business practices: Companies can shift their business models to prioritize sustainability, such as through sustainability reporting, adopting green technologies, and improving resource efficiency.
  • Consumer behavior: Individuals can contribute by making conscious choices, such as buying local and seasonal foods, saving energy, avoiding water waste, and recycling correctly.
  • Collaboration: A global, collaborative effort between administrations, businesses, and individuals is necessary to make a systemic shift towards sustainable systems.
